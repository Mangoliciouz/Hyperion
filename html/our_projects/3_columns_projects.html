  • Fibre Telecoms

    There are many reasons to consider using fibre optic connectivity to deliver data, such as Fibre can carry data much further than traditional copper technologies without degradation of service.. The technology is passive and does not suffer from interference from other sources, ideal to run alongside electrical services for example. For harsh environments such as the marine industry and shoreside services it is an ideal technology to deliver data either for data or broadband applications without degradation. Whilst the national service providers concentrate on upgrading cities and larger rural areas, Hyperion Connect specifically focusses on the more challenging to reach environments. If you or your business are suffering from poor connectivity then get in touch to discuss what options we can propose.

  • Copper Telecoms

    Traditional copper connectivity still is important for certain applications, usually when technology is co-located within a building or structure. Most technology still has ethernet ports however the specification has been increasing in recent years so existing cabling might not be suitable to carry the increased data loads now in demand. New phone systems and security cameras are now more likely to be IP based and demand ethernet connectivity, so extra cabling will be required. Whatever the requirement, Hyperion Connect can design, manage and install services to suit even the most demanding of applications.

  • Wireless Telecoms

    Wireless connectivity is a great technology to enable broadband connectivity to the most outlying of properties without digging grounds to reach the site, achieving river crossings or across challenging terrain for example. It is reasonably resilient (more so than satellite broadband) and Hyperion have experience of installations to the most sensitive of locations, minimalizing obvious technology, even painting the equipment to hide from view. Hyperion can also advise on hybrid systems, using fibre, copper and wireless in various combinations to overcome the most challenging of broadband connectivity requirement.
